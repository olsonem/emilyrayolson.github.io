Art Cards

Initial Planning Statement

"On February 7, 2017, The Metropolitan Museum of Art implemented a new policy known as Open Access, which makes images of artworks it believes to be in the public domain widely and freely available for unrestricted use, and at no cost, in accordance with the Creative Commons Zero (CC0)."

Since learning this news I have wanted to explore the Met's collection. My plan is to create a set of flashcards with images of art from the Met. I think it would be fun to have different sets of these cards, but I am going to start with one set, and see if it is scale-able for this project, or if I need to limit the project at this time.

My cards will have both a hover and a click feature. On the hover feature the title, date, artist, and medium will pop up, and on click the card will flip over giving more information about the specific painting. The page will be designed in a simple clean and responsive manner likely with a Bootstrap foundation. There will be ample space between the images to allow for resting places that will not trigger the hover feature. As a student making analog Art History flashcards was a major pain, and art history books are heavy to lug around. It would be so cool to be able to develop an idea like this into an app so that you could have access to study from your phone. I am worried about writing the JS, but I think I will be able to accomplish my goals.
